S-matrix formalism for computing Bogoliubov coefficients in particle production mechanism induced by gravitational inhomogeneities (see [	arXiv:2502.12249](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.12249)) and phenomenomenological early univrse applications in first-order phase transitions and preheating.

Folder with:
1. In [spin-J field massless](https://github.com/stefanobartolomei/Master-s-Thesis/tree/0ad66b397b9016f9642f8863cabd8c9a4f6aa7d9/spin-J%20field%20massless) and [spin-J field massive](https://github.com/stefanobartolomei/Master-s-Thesis/tree/0ad66b397b9016f9642f8863cabd8c9a4f6aa7d9/spin-J%20field%20massive) there are the calculations for Bogoliubov coefficients with S-matrix formalism used in QFT in Minkowski spacetime for both massless and massive fields with various spins. The kernel functions for the gravitational sources, parametrized in Newtonian gauge, are also computed.
2. In [spin-J field TT](https://github.com/stefanobartolomei/Master-s-Thesis/tree/3dcda6ddea87bf56f03662b5c7c356f348a2fb36/spin-J%20field%20TT) the kernels functions are computed using the effective field method and the results confirm the previous derivation.
3. Importantly, [spin-1 massive TT.nb](https://github.com/stefanobartolomei/Master-s-Thesis/blob/94c63233d05c4cf908d37c8feb015068e648f31e/spin-J%20field%20TT/spin-1%20massive%20TT.nb) applies those formulae to the phenoomenological scenario of tensor metric perturbations generated in a first-order phase transition. I estimate the present-day relic abundance of vector massive particles produced by these inhomogeneities and investigate the possibility that it reproduces the dark matter abundance. The template of the power spectrum for tensor modes generated by bubble collisions is taken by [arXiv:0901.1661](https://arxiv.org/abs/0901.1661v2).
4. In [PREHEATING AND GW](https://github.com/stefanobartolomei/Master-s-Thesis/blob/3fecf25d411b74a01a930392785a70fb52e1a837/PREHEATING%20AND%20GW.nb) I primarly study the preheating scenario costituted by inflaton field $\phi$ with potential $V(\phi) = \frac{1}{2}m_\phi^2 \phi^2$ with a scalar spectator field $\chi$ interacting through $g^2\phi^2\chi^2$ term. The code analyzes the behaviour of mode functions of the spectator field, the occupation number of particles. Moreover, it evaluates the power spectrum of tensor modes generated during preheating in both time-like and on-shell configurations. In conclusion, I apply these results to compute the relic abundance of conformal particles produced by these perturbation via the aforementioned particle production mechanism.
5. In [Definitions and Properties](https://github.com/stefanobartolomei/Master-s-Thesis/tree/3dcda6ddea87bf56f03662b5c7c356f348a2fb36/Definitions%20and%20properties) there are the definitions used in calculations for spinors, polarization vectors and tensors, with several consistency checks. Additionally, it contains checks for properties of field theory discussed.
