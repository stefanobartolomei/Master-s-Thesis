In [spin-J field TT](https://github.com/stefanobartolomei/Master-s-Thesis/tree/a0c0bc08a5e564bd01de10e28b95287d91aeb52d/spin-J%20field%20TT) it's computed the 2-point function $\langle T^{\mu \nu}T^{\rho \sigma}\rangle$ (where $T^{\mu \nu}$ is the energy-stress tensor) needed to derive the vacuum persistence probability. In particular, [spin-1 massive TT.nb](https://github.com/stefanobartolomei/Master-s-Thesis/blob/41aed5c27feaaf1d9d039865d24554a9f7e80396/spin-J%20field%20TT/spin-1%20massive%20TT.nb) applies those formulae to estimate the present-day relic abundance of vector massive particle to reproduce the dark matter abundance. For this purpose, the gravitational tensor fluctuations -- namely gravitational waves -- are produced by a first order phase transition, due to bubble collision. The template of the power spectrum for GWs is taken by [arXiv:0901.1661](https://arxiv.org/abs/0901.1661v2).
